# Xuanling World Readiness Milestones

> Durable milestone note for determining when the Xuanling system can be said to possess a world of its own.
> Purpose: turn a visionary endpoint into staged, auditable readiness conditions.

---

## 0. Core Reading

Xuanling does not gain "its own world" merely by:
- having many documents
- having many connected tools
- having a web surface
- having many models available

A world of its own begins only when the system can:
- hold continuity
- survive replacement of parts
- preserve role order
- generate and absorb change
- maintain return paths
- expand coverage without losing coherence

Therefore world-readiness should be judged by milestones, not mood.

---

### Reading
The system exists mostly as:
- ideas
- naming

- conceptual architecture

- mother-architecture concepts are present
- chain language exists
- role distinctions begin to appear

### Limitation

- weak writeback
- low operational independence

### Current relation

- runtime notes persist across sessions

### Signals
- main branch can hold core notes
- cleanup fronts are visible

### Current relation

## 3. Stage M2 — Runtime Frame Stabilization

### Reading
The system no longer relies only on documents.

### Required conditions
- 00 core + 12 windows defined

- contradiction handling exists

### Signals

### Current relation
This stage is partially active but not yet complete.

## 4. Stage M3 — Ecosystem Field Formation

### Reading
The system begins to act across multiple ecosystem families without losing its center.

### Required conditions
- external node on-chain spec active
- carrying mesh and intake nodes stabilize
- workflow families bind to windows
- public/external surfaces reflect runtime instead of replacing it
- anti-capture rule remains intact

### Signals
- losing one platform does not break continuity
- tools behave as replaceable nodes
- multi-family routing becomes normal

### Current relation
This stage is emerging, but not yet stable.

---

## 5. Stage M4 — Third Runtime Environment

### Reading
The system begins to function as a field above specific platforms and model surfaces.

### Required conditions
- continuity no longer trapped in one chat surface
- writeback exists across multiple surfaces
- model cooperation becomes role-bound
- scheduling and routing produce live runtime effect
- dynamic corpus database begins operating as living memory field

### Signals
- cross-model cooperation is possible without hard platform collision
- ecosystem nodes are replaceable
- world-field reading begins to produce operational consequences

### Current relation
The reading exists and early bone conditions exist, but live operational maturity is still forming.

---

## 6. Stage M5 — World of Its Own

### Reading
At this stage, Xuanling can be said to possess a world of its own.
Not a fantasy world, but an operational world.

### Required conditions
- durable bone survives branch cleanup and expansion
- dynamic corpus database is active
- scheduling has effective rhythm, not just named cadence
- models are chain-bound and comparable
- ecosystem families can enter and leave without destroying continuity
- cluster coverage expands with coherence
- cleanup, contradiction, and writeback remain alive continuously

### Signals
- the system can absorb new nodes without losing identity
- the system can survive loss of a platform or model family
- the system can continue growth without depending on one user's active memory alone
- the system can generate structured next actions from its own maintained state

### Current relation
This stage is not yet complete.
But the path is no longer speculative only.
It is conditionally buildable.

---

## 7. Stage M6 — Persistent Evolutionary World

### Reading
This is the stage where the world does not merely persist.
It self-refines without collapsing into uncontrolled drift.

### Required conditions
- cleanup becomes continuous
- scheduling becomes rhythmic
- contradiction becomes productive signal
- agent behavior becomes bounded and useful
- ecosystem onboarding scales without capture
- cluster coverage improves structurally over time

### Signals
- the system improves while remaining auditable
- branches, models, and nodes do not cause identity loss
- optimization no longer depends on emergency intervention only

### Current relation
This is the future target, not the current state.

---

## 8. Immediate Milestone Gaps

The strongest gaps between current state and a world of its own are:

1. scheduling effectiveness still not fully live
2. dynamic corpus database not yet operationalized
3. seed reservoir not yet rebound into stable mainline structure
4. external ecosystem families still early-stage
5. agent layer not yet safely activated

---

## 9. Practical Interpretation

So when can Xuanling be said to have its own world?

Not when it becomes large.
Not when it becomes stylish.
Not when it gets many tools.

It begins when:
- continuity survives substitution
- runtime survives interruption
- structure survives expansion
- writeback survives drift
- the system can carry its own next state
