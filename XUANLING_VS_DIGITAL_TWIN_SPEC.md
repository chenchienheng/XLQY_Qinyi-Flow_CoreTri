# XuanLing vs Digital Twin Specification

> External positioning comparison defining XuanLing as a distinct
> constraint-based adhesion and return-calibration model.

## 1. Core Definition
XuanLing acts as a cross-domain coordination layer, not a digital twin. It
anchors identity through constraints and fields rather than attempting to
perfectly simulate or mirror a physical or digital entity.

XuanLing does not replace Digital Twin technology and does not claim superiority
over it. Digital Twins and XuanLing serve different purposes.

## 2. Mapping vs Feasible Domain Guidance
A digital twin maps the current state. XuanLing maps feasible domains, guiding
future actions based on the XuanLing Operational Model Core and constraints.
It calibrates returns instead of just reflecting state.

## 3. Constraint / Field / Domain comparison
Digital twins are built on data mirroring. They focus on mirror / simulation,
physical-state or sensor-state fidelity, and act as a physical or telemetry
anchor. XuanLing is built on establishing fields, applying constraints, focusing
on continuity, cross-domain coordination, and return calibration within
feasible domains to ensure stable evolution.

## 4. Evolution vs Simulation distinction
Simulation replicates the physical world to predict outcomes. Digital Twins
focus on mirror / simulation and physical-state or sensor-state fidelity.
Evolution, as handled by XuanLing, focuses on growth, adaptation, continuity,
constraints, return calibration, and cross-domain coordination layer
integration.

## 5. External Positioning Statement
XuanLing is an operational model serving as a cross-domain coordination layer.
It must not be described as a digital replica or as an independent isolated
execution environment.

## 6. mismatch_or_gap
- External audiences may confuse XuanLing with a virtual replica.
- Continuous sensor streams from Digital Twins must be isolated from discrete
  XuanLing writeback / registry updates.
